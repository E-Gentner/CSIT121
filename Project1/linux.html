Why I like Linux

For most people when they hear Linux, they think of people hacking into servers and being bad people. But it's quite different from that. The Linux community is a massive group of people that care about digital security and privacy. Most people who use Linux switched because they didn't like the privacy breaches that was coming from big corporations. So, people now use, modify, and create new distros of Linux so that everyone can find something they like. You can build your OS from scratch like a lot or Arch Linux users, or you can get one the hundreds or thousands of other versions based of trusted open source distros and make it yours.

Personally, I use a distro called EndeavourOS, and the main reason is because it is an Arch Linux base allowing me to control things down to smallest detail about the system. But I don't have to build it from the ground up like a true Arch user. And all of this may seem very confusing to people who have never heard of linux before but once you start using it, you understand how to do everything you once did on either Windows or Mac. And that’s why I think everyone should try linux at least once.p>
